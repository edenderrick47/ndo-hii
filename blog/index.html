Should You Hire a Professional Editing Service for Your College Entrance Essay?

Whether you’ve always struggled to self-edit your own work or just want to gain a slight edge over other talented applicants, you may be considering the submission of your college entrance essay to an editor for a final polish. Read on to learn more about some of the factors you’ll want to consider when making this decision.

Using Your Own Words

The purpose of a college entrance essay is to allow you to jump off the paper for the admissions staff–that is, to provide some context for your GPA, standardized test scores, and the other information you’ll provide in your application. By writing an essay from the heart and explaining some situations you’ve overcome in your own life and what you hope to gain from the college experience at this specific college, you’ll provide admissions staff with a bit more information about your personality and goals, helping them make a better-informed decision.

While your entrance essay alone isn’t likely to make or break your chances at your dream school, a strong essay may provide the extra boost needed for a marginal student, while a poorly-written or offensive essay could disqualify an otherwise strong applicant. Because of this, it’s important to submit your best possible work.

However, ultimately you’ll want this work to be yours–which means, while the use of an editing service may be a good idea to ensure you’re submitting a strong piece of writing, having an essay written by someone else may leave you missing the point of this exercise entirely. It’s always best to ensure that the substance and content of your essay come straight from you, which can mean avoiding services that promise to do more than editing.

Other Considerations

Before soliciting the help of an editing service or doing anything more than having a parent, friend, or teacher give your essay a quick once-over for major errors, you’ll want to check your college’s admissions website to see whether there is any published policy on the use of editing services on admission essays.

While most colleges permit students to have their essays professionally edited, some schools will expressly prohibit these services, citing their desire to see the student’s raw capabilities without any outside help. By ensuring that you’re sticking to your college’s stated guidelines (if any), you can expect smooth sailing going forward.

Five Easy Steps to Retain What You Learn from Written Material

If you’re like most people, you may have difficulty remembering something you’ve learned. This is a common problem with not only students but professionals as well. There are several steps you can take to avoid late night cram sessions and be able to recall what you’ve learned quickly and easily. Although some of these steps may seem simplistic, they are rooted in much research and proven to be effective. While repetition certainly is useful for retaining information, there are far more effective methods that won’t bore you nearly as much.

The first tip is especially helpful for large quantities of information, similar to reading a chapter in a textbook. As you read through the text, break it down into bite-size chunks of information. Write the information you need to know on paper. Be certain you understand the information before moving onto the next section and if further analysis is needed, keep on the topic until you are confident it’s been mastered. Continue this practice until you are finished with the material.

The next step is to take the information you’ve learned from your notes and type it. This gives you a second level of exposure to the material and helps you recall how you interpreted the information you read. This step will also clean up the material and offer you another form of visual aid that is easy to read and concise. The typing of the material also slows down the reading process, so you are now reinforcing your previous exposure to the material.

Take the material you’ve typed and read it aloud. If you have a presentation to make, take an extra step and rehearse it in front of the mirror. Not only are you now building upon the previous steps by reciting the information yet again, but you’re adding the benefit of auditory learning just by hearing the material.

The last and perhaps most beneficial tip is to teach the material to someone else. Not only do you have to know and understand the material to accomplish this, it’s a commonly known learning theory that you’ll remember nearly 90 percent of what you teach to someone else. If you’re able to implement it, that adds additional benefit.

While these simple tips may seem like a bit of work, the effort is well worth the benefit. Not only are you building upon what you learn through repetition and recitation, each step beyond the initial reading and writing of the material builds upon the memorization by serving a useful purpose. These five easy steps will also prove to be much more effective and less daunting than simply reading the material over and over again, and will even aid you in putting the information to work for your benefit through thoughtful analysis.
